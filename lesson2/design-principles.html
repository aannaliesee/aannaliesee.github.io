Visual Hierarchy

Nikon
https://www.nikonusa.com/en/index.page

Visual hierarchy creates a pattern of attention to certain objects on a page. For this Nikon homepage, the camera lense is what first catches your eye, then the "Dreams defined" heading, then the images, and so on. This is a great example of controlling what the viewer's eyes see first. The lense is bold and center followed by smaller objects that are not as obvious.

Parc:Repetition

ThredUp
https://www.thredup.com/

ThredUp is an online clothing thrift store and advertise sales a lot. Here they used the element of repetition to be organized and eye-catching. Repetition looks clean and modern. They use a lot of this same sized circle throughout their page as well as the same two colors to create a repetitious pattern.

Clean Design and White Space

UnSplash
https://unsplash.com/

This website is a free image source where you can find almost any royalty-free picture. It is very minimal and clean. There is plenty of white space and all the colors are neutral which creates a clean look. The white space allows for viewers to focus on the images without being overwhelmed.
