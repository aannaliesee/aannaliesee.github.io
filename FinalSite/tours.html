Tours

Here are some stops on our island that you may want to visit with your scooter or jeep. There are many hidden gems, so don't feel the need to visit only these known attractions.

Santa Rosa Wall

A favorite spot in Cozumel, Santa Rosa Wall kind of has it all with healthy reefs that go down as deep as you're willing to venture (some typically hang around 70-80 ft.) and some really cool swim throughs! The dive ends on the sand where some are lucky enough to catch rays and larger fish like grouper hanging out. A must when diving Cozumel!

Faro Celerain Ecological Reserve

Faro Celarain (Punta Sur) is an Ecological Reserve,which encloses a variety of natural wonders. It covers an area of one thousand hectares and it is destined for the conservation and preservation of the 5 ecosystems that conform it. Faro Celarain is rich in coastal dunes, mangroves, reefs,lagoons systems, the Island's most beautiful beaches and reefs.

Playa de San Martin

Lovely white sand that stretch on for ages. There is a beach bar on the other side of the road with a few tourist stalls and washrooms. We were warned of the current, but the waves were nice.

El Mirador

Gorgeous spot. We went at sunset and watched the sun go down while enjoying the power of the waves. Very tranquil part of the island.

Cozumel Pearl Farm

Cozumel Pearl Farm is a family owned and operated pearl farm nestled in a beautiful white sand beach on the north shore of Cozumel Island. Being the only active Pearl Farm in the Caribbean, Cozumel Pearl Farm is located in a natural protected area that remains pristine and untouched; it is only accessible by boat since it is surrounded by the mangrove lagoon ecosystem on one side and the fascinating turquoise color waters of the Caribbean on the other.
